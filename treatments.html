Treatments

We offer personalized, painless, and reliable treatment options using the latest technologies for your oral and dental health.

Examination and X-Ray

When you arrive at our practice, we don't just focus on your complaint; we perform a comprehensive examination supported by radiological imaging. Thanks to these detailed inspections, we detect unnoticed problems early and create the most suitable and reliable treatment plan for you.

Periodontology (Gum Disease)

It aims to diagnose and treat diseases developing in the tissues surrounding the teeth and dental implants. Gum diseases cause the loss of surrounding structures such as gums and bone. The main cause involves microbial dental plaque. Removing this plaque is essential for both treatment and prevention.

Dental Fillings

These treatments aim to restore the function of teeth that have lost substance due to decay. After cleaning the decay, the tooth is filled with various materials. The filling procedure may vary depending on the extent of the decay. Local anesthesia is applied beforehand if deemed necessary.

Teeth Whitening

It involves lightening the color of teeth that have yellowed due to tea, coffee, acidic beverages, smoking, or biological factors by several shades using special chemical agents to achieve a whiter appearance.

Root Canal Treatment

This treatment deals with the tissues in the root portion of the tooth. The inside of the root is cleaned using specialized instruments. Depending on the condition, the treatment might be completed in a single session or span several sessions over a few weeks. Interrupting the treatment can delay recovery and decrease the success rate.

Orthodontics

It encompasses treatments to correct bite disorders, crowded teeth, and widely spaced teeth, aligning them properly within the dental arch using braces (brackets) or advanced clear aligners.

Tooth Extraction

Extracting untreatable teeth is crucial for protecting the remaining healthy tissues. Surgical extraction might be preferred for impacted teeth. These procedures are usually performed under local anesthesia, though conscious sedation or general anesthesia can be used if needed. Avoid smoking and alcohol, as they delay healing.

Surgical Impacted Tooth Extraction

For impacted teeth, a surgical extraction approach is preferred. These procedures are generally carried out under local anesthesia. However, when required, they can also be performed under conscious sedation or general anesthesia.

Dental Implants

Missing teeth can cause unbalanced forces on the jaw joints, leading to joint issues and the continuous melting of unused jawbone. Implants are a reliable treatment designed to mimic natural tooth roots, placed surgically into the jawbone under sterile conditions and local anesthesia, thereby preventing these problems.

Prosthetics

In cases of advanced tooth wear or missing teeth, fixed prostheses (like metal-supported porcelain or zirconium crowns) are used, which the patient cannot remove. Teeth must be reduced to a certain size for crowns or bridges. Additionally, removable prostheses are offered for completely edentulous patients if implants are not suitable.

Pediatric Dentistry

These practices aim to protect the primary and permanent teeth of children from infancy through adolescence. They include preventive measures such as protective sealants and fluoride applications to stop decay from developing.
